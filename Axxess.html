Axxess HomeCare Suite

In Summer 2016, I joined Axxess as a user experience design intern. I helped Axxess design new products as well as redesign flagship products to shape its future.

Axxess Nurse App Profile

When I started at Axxess, Axxess was working on a new mobile product to bridge the gap between patients and freelance nurses. My responsibility was to design user profile user interface for nurses to manage their personal and financial information (like Uber Driver's App). I need to make sure that the following user requirements were met:

1. Able to see and edit profile information.
2. Able to see their daily and weekly earnings.
3. Able to view ratings and detailed reviews.
4. Motivated to keep using and performing well.

After realizing the requirements for the design, I quickly streched out some design alternatives using Balsamiq Mockups. Then I showed the design alternatives to a few freelance nurses to learn which options were more intuitive and any other feedback from them. Here are some takeaways from the mini A/B test session:

With the user feedback in mind, the team got together to decide the design options are we going with. Then I prototyped the high-fidelity UI design with Sketch. After several rounds of feedback session with the management and potential users, here is the final design of the user profile:

User can see and edit their profile information as well as change their profile photos with a tap on the button

User can see their weekly earnings chart on the upper section and transactions sorted by date on the lower section

Users can see their overall ratings on the upper section and view the detailed reviews on the lower section

I designed a little gamification (achievement system) to encourage users to maintain good performance.

Onboarding Screens

The second project I worked on is the splash screens of the flagship mobile app new version. Our mobile team just added some exciting features to the app, and they couldn't wait to share the new features with the lovely users. On this project, I worked with both the marketing team and mobile development team to understand what new features are most important and what the best ways are to present the features with "Axxess personality".

After discussing with multiple teams and management, Another UX intern and I designed the splash screens using wireframes with descriptions that deliver a graphical illustration of our new features. We tested the design with our technical team and QA team to refine the information architecture and visual desig.

AgencyCore Redesign

While working on many new products and features, our team also redesigned the interface for Axxess's flagship product AgencyCore. This is the product Axxess designed in 2009. It helps Axxess stay connected with over 2000 homecare agencies and make 80% of revenue. However, when it was first designed, the main goal is just to "make it work". Let's take a look at its problems:

Customizable Dashboard Cards

While redesigning the product, we decided to use a card system. Instead of showing all information in tables, we used charts, icons and color labels to group and visualize information. Our design of the dashboard also allow rearrangement and customization.

Responsive Design - Mobile Friendly

According to the web traffic analysis, a big portion of the users access the product via mobile devices. However, our current framework doesn't support responsiveness. We decided to embrace the Bootstrap grid system when redesigning, so that users can always expect same level of experience.

Internal Training System

Once a new user signs up with Axxess, our implementation team will contact them to provide trainings. But currently, the implementation team uses Excel to track each user's training progress. Obviously the current approach isn't efficient and the information isn't transparent across all team members, so I started to design a system that allows our implementation team easily track each user's training progress and has as much automation as possible.

Internship Reflection

Since my background was in business operations, this was actually my first UX internship working at a company outside of campus. I really appreciate this opportunity to get my career off the ground. It was a time for both contributing and learning. I was implementing what I learned from school onto the designs I created as well as learning from the real world. Axxess is a company that has high standard for user experience. I enjoyed working with senior designers as well as other interns there. Whether you are looking for a full-time or internship position, Axxess is definitely what I recommend.
